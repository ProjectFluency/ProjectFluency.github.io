About Project Fluency

There are 23 million adults in the United States with limited English proficiency, 90% of them immigrants. For them, language limits day-to-day life, access to jobs they are otherwise skilled for, even the ability to help kids succeed in school.

The current solution, ESL (English as Second Language) courses, only enroll 4% of the population in need, and are notorious for poor quality--only 40% of ESL students make progress year-to-year. Technology-based language learning programs are designed for a very different audience--much more suitable to short-term travel rather than long-term life.

We need to do better.

Project Fluency's product helps immigrants working in service jobs turn everyday workplace interactions into learning opportunities. In order to do this, we need to create a feeling of safety so people with limited proficiency can begin having conversations in English. We help small groups self-administer a small curriculum focused on informal conversation, and augment it with SMS-based behavioral nudges, leadng to real-world interactions in English. The result is a positive feedback loop that improves both confidence and knowledge over time.
